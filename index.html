Energy Based Working

Energy Based Working (EBW) is a service design project for the Dutch Architecture Bureau Heembouw. The huge company office space needed a new sustainable design, stimulating activity based working which resulted in this challenging project objective: “Stimulate the employees of Heembouw to use the available variety in work spaces in such a way that it supports the well being of the employees while having a positive environmental impact.”

The EBW concept solves the complex design challenge through interactive gamification. The concept has a clean, simple and very engaging UX design, motivating its users to reach higher levels of sustainable office environment. The game blends easily into the everyday-life of employees by using familiar elements like budget and presents energy usage in a more tangible way by expressing consumption in CO2 points. It provides real-time feedback to increase awareness about energy usage and personal well-being. EBW engages its users by applying the principles of quantified self which make it fun, social and competitive.

The interactive EBW game will be part of the everyday worklife at the office. By using a personal EBW card every employee will check in at his workspot in a special card reader. Only after check-in the energy sources and devices in a room such as sockets, AC, lights or monitors will become available. Every user will be charged with CO2 points per minute for the period of working. At group meetings the energy consumption will be divided among the participants which encourages office space sharing. The Work Cafe at the Heembouw office is a point free zone where everyone can relax and get a break from the competition. Keeping better personal energy levels will be enabled by a special timer. Its purpose is to increase attention span among employees by making them set their own goals for a peroid of focused and uninterrupted working time. Following these goals along with every recycling attemp in the office will be rewarded with extra CO2 points.

Game statistics will be available on a personal dashboard. The dashboard will be part of an interactive system implemented in
the already existing Heembouw platform. Every employee will be provided with budget and is alone responsible and personally in charge for his CO2 points consumption. After every week employees will end up having an amount of points which will be transferred (positive or negative) to their savings account. Leaderboard will show real-time performance of every employee
in the ranklist based on their current budget points. At the end of every week the best players in the game will benefit by receiving different rewards from the company.

The Energy Based Working service design concept is brought to
life with a video prototype that presents the user flow and his interaction with all the touchpoints of the system.

Inspectory

Smart Society is a mega-trend in our modern world. It successfully harnesses the potential of digital technology, connected devices and the use of digital networks to improve people’s lives. Being led by the strong spirit of innovation the Dutch municipality of Delft is investing in student-driven projects to modernize its administrative services. The high-quality maintanence of public spaces has always been a difficult task, but smart technologies are now having the potential to change that.

Looking for innovative ideas and opportunities, the municipality of Delft assigned me and my fellow students with an interesting challenge:
Design a smart tool that manages and distributes work in public spaces in a simple and accessible way! This is how the project "Inspectory" was born.

"Inspectory" is a mobile design project aiming at supporting public space management inspectors in their daily tasks. Currently inspectors are having an old-established work flow. They get alerted from citizens about public space issues. Every signal they receive have to be inspected personally. Then inspectors have to return to the municipality, write a report about the problem and assign the right maintanence comapany with the task to solve it. This is a long procedure that requires quite a lot of effort. Gemeente Delft has recently invested in tablet devices that should digitalyze the administrative process, but still needs a special mobile application. It should enable inspectors to write reports and assign tasks to maintanence contractors on the spot, saving a lot of time and effort.

Designing for administrative processes is quite difficult taking into account the many diverse and often unclear requirements. However the current design of the Inspectory mobile application gets a lot of positive feedback from the client and its future potential users.

Main design requirement for the mobile application is the presence of a clear and exact map of the city, navigating inspectors to different locations. The map is a key design element. It must present different public space problems with pinpoints and signalize the exact stage of their work progress. In addition to that, inspectors must be provided with an easy reporting system. Having a categorized overview of all the different current problems is another important requirement for the app.

The current UI of the app succeeds in meeting those requirements. Recent usability tests revealed that inspectors are interacting with the system very naturally. They gave positive feedback about the information architecture and the aestetics. They believe Inspectory is exactly what they need and cant wait to start using it.

Inspectory is a unique project, because it translates the unpleasent administrative job into a desired and valuable digital user experience!

Design challenge

Design an innovative mutual fund investment platform with focus on usabillity and usefullness, including an advanced robo-advisory service that stimulates investments and minimizes risk.

Monett is an ambitious Romanian FinTech start-up for online distribution of mutual funds. It is aiming at creating a sophisticated personal wealth management digital service.

The start-up is a collaboration between the Bulgarian software development companany "BG Web Soft Ltd." and the Romanian investment intermediary "Goldring SSIF". The product will be launched for the Romanian financial market.

As Monett's product designer, my main goal is to make the product useful, usable, efficient and aesthetically pleasing. It's design should guide and assist the user on every single step of his digital interaction with the app.

Monett is basically a mutual fund trading platform and as such it is essential for the application to present the performance of the user's investments in a digital portfolio and offer a marketplace for all the distributed mutual funds. There should be detailed information about the benefits and risks of every possible investment.

Users can make single direct investments in mutual funds or start their own investment plans with monthly installments according to their preference.

In order for Monett to be the best product of its kind on the market, it is going to offer a very sophisticated Robo Advisor. It will help users make the best investment decisions according to their pre-measured investment profile. The Robo Advisor will prevent users from making risky investments and help them maximize their profits.

Monett is about money, not art! It is about increasing personal wealth!

Postbank Mobile Banking

Design challenge

Redesign the existing mobile banking app of Postbank by taking user-centered design decisions, based on user research and the latest UX design trands and practices.

I recently worked on a project for redesign of the Postbank Mobile Banking Application for Bulgaria. Based on my design concept and experiential prototype my current employer is now in advanced stage of negotiations for developing the bank's new mobile banking app.

I conducted an in-depth user research to identify the problems of the current design. It turned out that Postbank mobile banking users are in general satisfied with their mobile banking experience. The results of my research showed that users are experiencing some difficulties with the visual appearance of the way their bank accounts are linked to their cards. The procedure for ordering a money transfer between accounts was stated as unnecessary complicated, especially the transfers between own accounts. An interesting finding was the missing option for easily requesting a payment from another user.

After carefully analyzing my research findings I created a design concept with focus on the user and his needs.

The redesinged mobile banking app is essentially based on the existing application. It includes many new features such as virtual cards, NFC payments option, payment request generator with QR code and also access to the bank's investment services.

Raiffeisenbank
Desktop Trading Platform

Design challenge

Design a simple, accessible and desirable trading platform with focus on novice users, stimulating as many investment operations as possible.

I am currently working on a project for design of a desktop trading platform for Raiffeisen Bank Bulgaria. The product is intended to provide the bank clients with an access to the financial markets around the world.

Financial trading is getting more and more popular every year among greater audience of non-proffessional investors. This is why Raifeissen Bank Bulgaria wants to offer its clients a new simple, accesible and less detailed investment service that is focused more on the novice users. The Desktop Trading Platform will be powered by the software product "Finasyst" of the Bulgarian company "BG Web Soft Ltd."

Users will be given the opportunity to trade stocks, etfs, gold and forex. The investment details related to technical and financial data and analysises are limited to the legaly allowed minimum. This makes the app appear more simple, accesible and desirable. The design is emphasizing on the most trendy and popular stocks from the american market.

The platform provides easy access to "Buy" and "Sell" order requests from almost every screen. This design decision is taken with the intent to stimulate more order requests. Novice users usually tend to make impulsive and emotional investment decisions and by lowering the commision fees, but icreasing the amount of investments the bank is planning to earn good profit.

This project is in early stages of negotiations with Raiffeisenbank, but the concept already received positive and valuable feedback.

Parking SMS prototype

Design challenge

Design a mobile app for paying in city parking zones via SMS and cards with focus on desirability and ease of use.

In the summer of 2020 I was assigned with a project for design of a new mobile app for parking payments in big Bulgarian cities.

Parking SMS is my design concept for an app that allows you to pay public parking easily with features like detecting your location and sending the appropriate short text message (SMS) to the corresponding service numbers. Payments are also possible via bank cards.

You can create a profile for each one of your cars and send parking messages with a single tap. Additionally, you can share your parking payments requests via social media and send parking messages for other people.

Available parking zones are suggested automatically, based on your location. Parking position is detected and displayed on the map in the application along with the nearby parking zone bounds.

Parking SMS is a fresh and easy to use app that makes your city parking issues way less complicated.
